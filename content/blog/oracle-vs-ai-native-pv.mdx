Oracle Argus Safety processes 10 million adverse event cases annually and is deeply embedded in pharma operations worldwide. So why would a startup challenge it? Because the architecture is fundamentally different.

## The Architecture Gap

Argus was designed before AI existed. Safety One Intake layers AI on top as a workflow automation. SafetySignal was designed with AI as the engine — causality assessment, signal prioritization, narrative generation, and benefit-risk evaluation are all AI-native, not bolt-ons.

SafetySignal-PV-7B v3 achieves 80% agreement with Claude Sonnet 4 on causality assessments, with an average Naranjo score difference of 1.0 — within clinical acceptable range for a 7.6B parameter model.

## The Signal Prioritization Gap

Oracle Empirica detects statistical signals using PRR and ROR. SafetySignal's Sprint 60 composite Priority Score combines disproportionality, causality confidence, clinical severity, case volume, and novelty into one ranked queue. The result: a list a PV scientist can act on immediately.

## Listedness Determination

In Oracle Argus, determining whether an AE is listed in product labeling is manual. SafetySignal Sprint 62 automates it entirely — cross-referencing signals against structured labeling data and generating the 15-day expedited reporting decision in real time.

- Amoxicillin anaphylactic reaction: UNLISTED → `15-DAY REPORT REQUIRED`
- Relacorilant hepatotoxicity: LISTED → routine reporting

## Benefit-Risk

Oracle has no AI-native benefit-risk assessment. SafetySignal Sprint 66 generates BRAT evaluations with weighted benefit scores, risk scores, B/R ratios, and net clinical benefit figures from case data in under 50ms. A true first-in-class capability.

## Deployment Reality

Oracle Argus takes 12–18 months and millions to deploy. SafetySignal is API-first, containerized, and can be live in days. For pharma companies that need PV capability without the enterprise software tax, that's a fundamentally different proposition.
